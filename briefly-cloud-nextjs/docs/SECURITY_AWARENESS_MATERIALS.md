## Monthly Security Tips

### January: New Year, New Security Habits
- **Tip**: Start the year by updating all your passwords
- **Action**: Enable MFA on all accounts that support it
- **Focus**: Password hygiene and account security

### February: Love Your Data (Data Privacy Month)
- **Tip**: Review what personal data you're sharing online
- **Action**: Clean up old accounts and unused services
- **Focus**: Data privacy and digital footprint

### March: Spring Cleaning for Security
- **Tip**: Update all software and remove unused applications
- **Action**: Review and revoke unnecessary app permissions
- **Focus**: Software updates and system maintenance

### April: Phishing Season is Here
- **Tip**: Be extra vigilant about suspicious emails
- **Action**: Report any phishing attempts to the security team
- **Focus**: Email security and phishing awareness

### May: Mobile Security Matters
- **Tip**: Secure your mobile devices with strong PINs/biometrics
- **Action**: Review app permissions on your phone
- **Focus**: Mobile device security

### June: Safe Travels (Travel Security)
- **Tip**: Use VPN when connecting to public Wi-Fi
- **Action**: Enable remote wipe on all devices
- **Focus**: Travel and remote work security

### July: Social Media Safety
- **Tip**: Review privacy settings on social media accounts
- **Action**: Think twice before posting work-related content
- **Focus**: Social engineering prevention

### August: Back-to-School Security
- **Tip**: Educate family members about online safety
- **Action**: Set up parental controls and safe browsing
- **Focus**: Family and personal security

### September: Backup Your Life
- **Tip**: Ensure all important data is backed up
- **Action**: Test your backup and recovery procedures
- **Focus**: Data backup and recovery

### October: Cybersecurity Awareness Month
- **Tip**: Take the annual security training refresher
- **Action**: Participate in security awareness activities
- **Focus**: Comprehensive security review

### November: Thankful for Security
- **Tip**: Be grateful for security measures that protect you
- **Action**: Thank your security team for their hard work
- **Focus**: Security culture and appreciation

### December: Holiday Security
- **Tip**: Be cautious of holiday-themed phishing emails
- **Action**: Secure online shopping with strong passwords
- **Focus**: Holiday and shopping security

## Security Awareness Videos (Scripts)

### Video 1: "The Phishing Email" (2 minutes)

**Scene 1**: Employee receives urgent email from "CEO"
- Email: "URGENT: Need you to purchase gift cards for client meeting"
- Red flags: Generic greeting, urgent tone, unusual request

**Scene 2**: Employee almost clicks but remembers training
- Checks sender address: ceo@rekonnlabs.co (fake domain)
- Hovers over links without clicking
- Notices poor grammar and spelling

**Scene 3**: Employee reports to security team
- Forwards email to phishing@rekonnlabs.com
- Security team confirms it's a phishing attempt
- Company-wide alert sent to warn others

**Lesson**: Always verify unexpected requests, especially urgent ones involving money or sensitive information.

### Video 2: "The Tailgater" (90 seconds)

**Scene 1**: Employee enters office building with badge
- Stranger follows closely behind without badging in
- Employee holds door open to be polite

**Scene 2**: Stranger gains access to sensitive areas
- Walks around office freely
- Takes photos of computer screens
- Accesses unlocked workstations

**Scene 3**: Security discovers the breach
- Reviews security footage
- Identifies the unauthorized person
- Traces entry back to tailgating incident

**Lesson**: Always ensure people badge in individually, even if it seems rude to let the door close.

### Video 3: "The USB Drop" (2 minutes)

**Scene 1**: Employee finds USB drive in parking lot
- Curious about contents
- Plugs into work computer to see what's on it

**Scene 2**: Malware activates
- Computer starts behaving strangely
- Files begin encrypting (ransomware)
- Network access spreads the infection

**Scene 3**: IT responds to incident
- Isolates infected systems
- Begins recovery procedures
- Investigates the source

**Lesson**: Never plug unknown USB devices into work computers. Report found devices to security.

## Interactive Security Quizzes

### Quiz 1: Phishing Detection

**Question 1**: You receive an email from your bank asking you to verify your account by clicking a link. What should you do?
a) Click the link immediately to secure your account
b) Call your bank directly to verify the request
c) Reply to the email asking for more information
d) Forward the email to your colleagues

**Answer**: b) Call your bank directly to verify the request

**Question 2**: Which of these is a red flag in an email?
a) Personalized greeting with your name
b) Company logo and branding
c) Urgent action required within 24 hours
d) Professional email signature

**Answer**: c) Urgent action required within 24 hours

### Quiz 2: Password Security

**Question 1**: What makes a strong password?
a) Your pet's name + birth year
b) 12+ characters with mixed case, numbers, and symbols
c) The same password for all accounts for consistency
d) A simple word that's easy to remember

**Answer**: b) 12+ characters with mixed case, numbers, and symbols

**Question 2**: How often should you change your passwords?
a) Every 30 days
b) Every 90 days
c) Only when there's a security breach
d) Never, if they're strong and unique

**Answer**: c) Only when there's a security breach (modern guidance)

### Quiz 3: Physical Security

**Question 1**: Someone you don't recognize asks you to hold the door open for them. What should you do?
a) Hold the door open to be polite
b) Ask them to badge in themselves
c) Let the door close and walk away
d) Call security immediately

**Answer**: b) Ask them to badge in themselves

**Question 2**: When leaving your desk, you should:
a) Leave your computer unlocked for quick access
b) Lock your screen (Win+L or Cmd+Ctrl+Q)
c) Turn off your computer completely
d) Ask a colleague to watch your desk

**Answer**: b) Lock your screen (Win+L or Cmd+Ctrl+Q)
